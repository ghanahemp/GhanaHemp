Who We Are

GhanaHemp.com is Ghana's leading independent news and information platform dedicated to the hemp and cannabis industry. We launched in 2026 — the same year Ghana's Interior Minister officially opened the Cannabis Regulatory Programme — to provide Ghanaian farmers, entrepreneurs, investors, and the general public with accurate, verified, and timely information about this fast-moving sector.

We are not affiliated with NACOC, the Ministry of the Interior, or any cannabis licence holder. We are an independent publication. Our only obligation is to our readers.

Our Mission

Ghana's cannabis regulatory framework — Act 1100 (2023) and L.I. 2475 (2023) — created enormous opportunity for Ghanaian farmers and entrepreneurs. But the information landscape is confusing, fast-changing, and full of misinformation. Unlicensed intermediaries are already exploiting applicants. NACOC has had to issue formal public warnings.

GhanaHemp.com exists to cut through that noise. Our mission is simple:

  • Provide accurate, verified information about NACOC licensing — free, always
  • Cover Ghana cannabis news with the seriousness it deserves
  • Explain complex policy and science in plain language
  • Amplify Ghana's hemp opportunity to international investors and partners
  • Hold the industry and regulators accountable through independent journalism

Our Editorial Standards

Every article published on GhanaHemp.com is held to the following standards:

  • Verified sources only. Regulatory information is sourced directly from NACOC (ncc.gov.gh) and the Ministry of the Interior (mint.gov.gh). We do not publish unverified claims about licences, fees, or legal requirements.
  • Original writing. We do not republish press releases or copy from other outlets. Every article is written in our own voice with our own analysis.
  • Clear legal disclaimers. We always remind readers that recreational cannabis is illegal in Ghana, that our content is not legal advice, and that all licensing applications must go through NACOC directly — never through intermediaries.
  • No commercial relationships with licence applicants. We do not accept payment to promote specific companies, licence holders, or applicants. Our editorial is fully independent.
  • Corrections policy. If we publish incorrect information, we correct it promptly and transparently. Contact us at contact page.

Important: GhanaHemp.com is an independent news publication. We are not NACOC, we are not affiliated with the Ministry of the Interior, and we cannot process or assist with licence applications. Always apply directly at portal.ncc.gov.gh.
