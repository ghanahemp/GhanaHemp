Ghana Officially Launches Cannabis Regulatory Programme, Begins Medicinal and Industrial Cannabis Licensing

Ghana's Historic Cannabis Regulatory Programme Launch

Ghana has taken a significant step forward in establishing a modern regulatory framework for cannabis cultivation and processing. On February 26-27, 2026, the Interior Minister officially launched the country's Cannabis Regulatory Programme, marking a pivotal moment in Ghana's approach to controlled medicinal and industrial cannabis cultivation. This launch activates a comprehensive legal framework that positions Ghana as a leader in West African cannabis regulation and opens new economic opportunities for Ghanaian citizens.

The programme represents a carefully structured approach to cannabis legalization, focusing exclusively on low-THC cannabis varieties for medicinal and industrial purposes. The National Cannabis Commission (NCC), operating under the regulatory framework established by Legislative Instrument 2475 (L.I. 2475), has been tasked with overseeing the licensing process and ensuring strict compliance with Ghana's cannabis regulations.

The Cannabis Regulatory Programme demonstrates Ghana's commitment to balancing economic development with public health and safety through controlled, regulated cultivation of low-THC cannabis.

Ghana Interior Ministry Statement

Licensing Framework and Requirements

The regulatory programme introduces a comprehensive licensing system with 11 distinct categories designed to accommodate various aspects of the cannabis supply chain. These categories span cultivation, processing, distribution, export, and related activities, allowing Ghanaian entrepreneurs to participate at multiple levels of the industry.

A critical requirement of the Cannabis Regulatory Programme is that all licenses are limited exclusively to Ghanaian citizens and Ghanaian-registered entities. This protects local economic interests and ensures that the benefits of the emerging cannabis industry remain within Ghana. Applicants must meet stringent licensing requirements, including demonstrating operational capacity, security measures, and compliance with health and safety standards.

The National Narcotics Control Board (NACOC) works in coordination with the NCC to ensure that licensing holders maintain strict compliance with regulated cultivation standards. All interested applicants can access the official licensing portal at portal.ncc.gov.gh, where they can submit applications and obtain detailed information about specific license categories and requirements.

Key Features of the Framework

The regulatory framework emphasizes transparency, security, and accountability throughout the cannabis cultivation and processing chain. Holders of cultivation licenses must adhere to strict protocols regarding plant genetics, cultivation practices, and THC content monitoring. Processing licenses enable value-added activities, while export licenses allow qualified operators to supply international medicinal cannabis markets under Ghana's regulatory oversight.

What This Means for Ghana

Ghana's launch of its Cannabis Regulatory Programme represents a transformative moment for the nation's agricultural and pharmaceutical sectors. The programme creates legitimate employment opportunities and attracts investment in a growing global industry, while maintaining strict government control through the NCC and NACOC oversight.

For Ghanaian citizens, the exclusive citizenship requirement ensures local participation and ownership of cannabis enterprises. The 11 licensing categories provide multiple pathways for entrepreneurs to contribute to a sustainable, regulated industry. The programme also positions Ghana as an international supplier of quality medicinal cannabis, potentially generating significant export revenue.

This regulatory approach allows Ghana to benefit from the global medicinal cannabis market while maintaining public health standards and preventing illicit cultivation. As implementation proceeds through portal.ncc.gov.gh, Ghana's cannabis regulatory framework will serve as a model for responsible cannabis legalization in Africa.

Recreational cannabis remains strictly illegal in Ghana. This article covers the legal industrial hemp and medicinal cannabis sector only, governed by Act 1100 (2023) and L.I. 2475 (2023). All licensed activities require a valid NACOC licence. Apply only at portal.ncc.gov.gh. This is not legal advice.
